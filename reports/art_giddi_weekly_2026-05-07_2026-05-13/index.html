Business context

  • Business: GIDDI is a direct-to-consumer brand making high-quality prostate massagers (Thor, Tomo II are the highest-margin SKUs); premium positioning, motion-tech-led, US-only ad spend on a Google Ads account managed in-house. Per BC last set 2026-03-29.
  • Economics: AOV $130, 75% gross margin, ~$97.50 gross profit per order. LTV and repeat-purchase rate are flagged unknown in BC — the dollar translation in this recap uses AOV as the proxy and names the assumption.
  • Goal this period: scale while improving performance. Targets: $65 CPA on Google Ads, 2.0× ROAS minimum (3.0× aspirational, blended), $30K monthly budget (flexible).
  • Constraint: sensitive-category (no Display, no remarketing, limited audience targeting) — the lever set is Search-only.
  • Seasonality: mid-May is non-peak; BC names Q4 / Black Friday as the peak windows. Period-over-period framing is uncomplicated by seasonality this week.

Headline

The week missed both targets and reversed last week's direction. CPA closed at $111.75 against the $65 target (a 72% overshoot) and ROAS at 1.16× against the 2.0× floor (a 42% shortfall). Both metrics regressed week-over-week — CPA was $89.91 prior week, ROAS was 1.43×. The week's run rate ($1,899.74 / 7d) extrapolates to roughly $8.1K / month, well under the $30K cap; with BC's growth-priority lens, the account is under-pacing the budget envelope and the per-conversion economics, not the spend envelope, are the constraint to fix.

One bright signal inside the week: Wednesday May 13 delivered 5 conversions on $212.56 spend (CPA $42.51, ROAS 3.90×) — the only day inside both targets, driven by TG_Search_Beginner (2 conv on $69.89) and Branded (2 conv on $38.48). That is the shape the account needs to hold for seven days, not one.
